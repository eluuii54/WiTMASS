Core Boxing Fundamentals

Before learning combinations and advanced techniques, every boxer must master the four pillars of boxing: stance, guard, footwork and breathing. These fundamental skills form the foundation of balance, defense, movement and endurance inside the ring. Professional champions spend countless hours perfecting these basics because strong fundamentals lead to better speed, accuracy and overall performance during a fight.

The interactive cards below introduce each of these essential concepts. Take your time to understand them, as every successful boxer begins by mastering the basics before progressing to powerful punches and advanced strategies.

Your Boxing Journey

From Beginner to Champion

Stance

A proper stance helps a boxer stay balanced, move quickly and defend effectively.

Guard

The guard protects the face and body while keeping the boxer ready to attack or counter.

Footwork

Footwork allows a boxer to control distance, create angles and avoid punches.

Breathing

Controlled breathing improves endurance, focus and punching rhythm.

Punch Library

Jab Easy

A fast straight punch used to measure distance and set up combinations.

Cross Medium

A powerful straight punch usually thrown with the rear hand.

Hook Medium

A curved punch aimed from the side, often used at close range.

Uppercut Advanced

An upward punch used when the opponent is close or leaning forward.

Training Levels

Beginner

Focuses on stance, guard, basic punches and simple movement.

Intermediate

Develops combinations, defensive reactions, speed and conditioning.

Advanced

Includes sparring strategy, ring control, counters and fight preparation.
